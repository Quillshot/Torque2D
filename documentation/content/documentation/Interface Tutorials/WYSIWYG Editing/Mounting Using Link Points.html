Mounting

In order to mount objects using link points, you must first set up the link point(s) on the destination object.

Select your object and click the left chain icon above it (as shown in Figure 1).
Figure 1

Once you click the widget, the Linkpoint Editor will appear (Shown in Figure 2).

The main window shows your object with a graph overlay. If you click anywhere on your object or within the graph, you will create a new link point.

The blue-green outline around your image represents the bounds of your scene object.
Figure 2

As you create mount points, they will be added to list (as shown in Figure 3).
Figure 3

As an alternative to clicking on yoru image, you can create new points using the New Linkpoint button(as shown in Figure 4).
Figure 4

You can zoom in and out of the editing view by moving the Area slider (as shown in Figure 5).
Figure 5

Next to the Area slider, you will find 2 radio buttons (as shown in Figure 6) that control the ratio aspect of your editing area.

Assuming we stretched our object before editing its linkpoints, you can see its actual ratio using "Scene Aspect Ratio."

By selecting "Square Aspect Ratio" you will be modifying the linkpoints using the objects original square shape, which might look stretched (as shown in Figure 7).
Figure 6


Figure 7


The final set of buttons is in the lower right (Shown in Figure 8). The green arrows allow you to undo and redo your latest action, just like any word or paint editor.

Cancel will discard your changes and return back to your game editing. Save does the opposite by keeping your linkpoint changes and returning to your main scene graph.
Figure 8

To mount an object to the selected link point, first select the object you want to mount.

Next, click the Mount button above the particle effect (as shown in Figure 8).
Figure 9

When you click this button, the chosen object will jump to your mouse and move with it. You can see the yellow crosshair where your link point was placed. If you drag your mouse close to the link point, the object will snap to that point. Click to mount your object there (as shown in Figures 10 and 11).
